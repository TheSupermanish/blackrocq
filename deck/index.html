The problem

On a public chain, everyone sees
everything you do.

Every transfer, every swap, every resting order is public the instant you make it. Your positions leak, your orders get front-run, your strategy gets copied.

On a public DEX, posting an order is broadcasting your intent to every bot in the mempool. That is not a bug in one app. It is how public ledgers work.

What leaks today

Three things you can't keep private.

Transfers

Every send is a public trail.

Balances, counterparties, and timing are exposed forever. Anyone can map who paid whom, and how much.

Swaps

Front-run before they land.

A swap sits in the public mempool. Bots sandwich it, taking the spread and handing you a worse price.

Orders

A resting order is a signal.

Post a limit order and the whole market sees your size and price. They trade against you before you fill.

On public chains, privacy is not a missing feature. It is impossible. That is the gap.

Why this can only work on Canton

Two capabilities. Both load-bearing.

Capability 01

Sub-transaction privacy

Through the signatory / observer model, an outsider never learns a send, swap, or order happened. Confidentiality is a property of the ledger, not a feature bolted on top.

// market.query(Order) → ∅
Capability 02

Atomic multi-party settlement

A swap moves both legs together or not at all. There is no public mempool window to front-run, and no intermediary ever holds both sides.

// Fill: asset⇄asset in one tx, or neither
Take either away and confidential DeFi collapses. That is the test of a real Canton app.
